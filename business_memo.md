# Business Memo
## To: Retention Team
### Subject: Key Findings from Customer Churn Analysis
The exploratory analysis identified several factors that appear to influence customer churn.

### Key Findings
1. Customers with low website activity show a greater likelihood of churning.
2. Customers who have not visited the platform recently are at higher risk.
3. Negative customer-support experiences are associated with increased churn.
4. Loyalty programme members appear to be more likely to remain active customers.
5. Churn behaviour varies across acquisition channels.

### Recommendations
1. Prioritize outreach to inactive customers before they become dormant.
2. Improve customer-support response quality and resolution speed.
3. Expand loyalty programme adoption among eligible customers.
4. Launch targeted re-engagement campaigns for customers with declining activity.
5. Review acquisition channels with higher churn rates and optimize marketing spend accordingly.

### Next Steps
The identified risk factors should be incorporated into customer segmentation and churn prediction models to improve retention effectiveness.
